Risk Event: External System Change (Week 8)

What Happened

During the filming phase, the external customer sign-up system that sales reps used in the field changed completely. Training content that had already been written, filmed, and entered post-production was now obsolete.

Cascading Impact

  • Filmed video content for affected modules was unusable
  • Filming location had to be re-booked for an additional day to maintain visual consistency — adding unplanned cost and scheduling pressure
  • Head salesperson needed field time to learn the new system before new content could be created
  • All rework had to happen within the remaining project window

Risk Response

  • Assessed blast radius — identified exactly which modules were affected vs. untouched
  • Shipped an MVP — created a voiceover screen-walkthrough as interim training, protecting the launch date
  • Re-booked the location — secured the same filming space to maintain visual consistency
  • Overtime + knowledge building — head salesperson field-tested the new system; team worked overtime to produce new content
  • Negotiated a 1-week extension — for the full polished version only

Outcome: The onboarding cohort launched on schedule with the MVP voiceover walkthrough. The full polished version was delivered one week later, maintaining quality standards without sacrificing the hard deadline.

Risk Management Framework

How I approach any new risk introduced to a project:

1

Assess Critical Path

Does this risk affect the launch date? What's the blast radius across workstreams?

2

Identify Recovery Options

Fast-track, MVP/phase, add resources, or trade scope elsewhere to absorb the impact.

3

Communicate Transparently

Present the risk, impact, and recommended response together. No surprises.

4

Protect the Date

The launch date is the anchor. Flex scope or resources — never the deadline.

5

Document & Adapt

Update the plan, log the decision, and communicate revised expectations.
